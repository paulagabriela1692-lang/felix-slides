Task 1

What I see in the campaigns
— and what I'd change

My framework

When I evaluate a remittance campaign, I look at things like: the right spokesperson, the right cultural codes (language, music, food, setting), trust signals (bank logos, testimonials), a clear value proposition vs. competitors, and a direct path to action on WhatsApp.

Analysis

01
Relevance
Built for Mexico, stretched everywhere else.
  • Smart move: Félix is using recognized personalities to borrow credibility in a trust-driven category. Caterine Ibargüen is the right call for Colombia; El Perro Bermúdez doesn't translate to DR or CO. The principle works — the talent just needs to fit each audience.
  • "I See Colombia" is the gold standard — Caterine Ibargüen, vallenato, Colombian accent, coffee. DR doesn't have an equivalent yet.
  • Shared emotional themes do land (immigrant experience, family bond, Día de la Madre) — but music, food, spokesperson and in-app screens are all Mexico-coded.
  • SDFC fútbol works for CO (Colombian player + sport), still needs tropicalization. For DR it misses — baseball is identity, not soccer.
02
Conversion
Strong branding, weak conversion.

Spots build awareness but don't drive action. The value proposition is missing.

What to add
  • Lead with the value prop: price, free first transfer, speed (money in minutes), ease (just WhatsApp — no app, no forms, no lines).
  • Show payout logos: Bancolombia, Davivienda, Éxito (CO); Banreservas (DR — plus Caribe Express when it launches). The fastest way to earn trust.
  • SEO captions + WhatsApp link in every description. Use high-intent terms: "enviar dinero a Colombia," "remesas a República Dominicana," "mandar dinero por WhatsApp."

Where my view differs between Dominicans and Colombians

🇩🇴 Dominicans
  • Original creative built specifically for them — not adaptations.
  • Use the right cultural codes: dembow/bachata/merengue, baseball (Juan Soto), Washington Heights.
  • Trust beats inspiration. They want safety, not aspiration.
  • Dominican spokesperson + testimonials.
  • Show people picking up money in cash at Banreservas or current cash-pickup partners (Caribe Express once it launches).
🇨🇴 Colombians
  • "I See Colombia" already works — the Caterine Ibargüen concept is a strong base.
  • Make the value props explicit: instead of just saying "rápido," show exact delivery time, price, and benefits (free first transfer, low commission). Plus visible payout logos (Bancolombia, Davivienda, Éxito).
  • Younger, digital-native audience — fútbol, música and lifestyle creators convert well.
  • Big opportunity: expand to other regions (costeños from Barranquilla/Cartagena, valluneos from Cali, rolos from Bogotá) so every Colombian in the U.S. sees their region reflected.

Deep Dive 1 · Highest-impact initiative

Influencers & creators

Why this is the deep dive

Creators unlock all three goals: acquisition (reach the right audience fast), first transaction ("people I follow use this" gets past the trust barrier), and second transaction (content throughout the 30 days keeps users sending). Plus their content powers paid and social channels.

The talent mix

Macro creators for reach and credibility (sports figures, TV personalities, beauty queens) + micro-to-mid-tier creators for hyper-local targeting (neighborhood accounts, community voices, lifestyle creators, immigration voices). The two layers work together: macro builds awareness, micro builds trust. Both feed paid and social.

Month 1 · Source & onboard

Find the right voices

  • Source talents — check demographics, engagement, audience location, sentiment, brand safety.
  • Pitch, negotiate contracts.
  • Negotiate content packages (videos [Reels/TikTok] + Stories) with one deliverable per week. Extends the campaign, spreads reach across different days, improves tracking, and gives us multiple touchpoints to drive a second transaction.
  • Write briefs tailored to each community: highlight Félix's value (price, speed, free first transfer, ease), include a strong CTA driving to WhatsApp, and trackable links for every post, Story and bio (if applicable).
  • Schedule and post across all their platforms (IG, TikTok, YouTube, Facebook), with co-branded collabs alongside the social media team when applicable.
Month 2 · Test & scale

Double down on winners

  • Identify the top creators per corridor driving actual transactions.
  • Test with Spark Ads first before graduating to full paid campaigns.
  • Reuse creator content for paid ads across all platforms (Meta, TikTok, YouTube, and more).
  • Reuse creator content on the website (testimonials, landing pages) and in targeted email campaigns.
  • Sign the best performers to longer deals (3-6 months) to build consistency.
Month 3 · Expand & retain

Ongoing campaign, wider reach

  • Launch the micro-creator affiliate program — pay per new customer, not per post.
  • Keep the campaign running with rotating new creators each month to expand reach and hit new audience segments.
  • Re-engage only high-conversion creators from previous months — not the ones who mostly drove views.
  • Layer creator-referenced WhatsApp nudges and automatic monthly sends (especially DR) to drive second transactions.

What I own

  • Strategy & sourcing: creator scouting, recruitment, contracts, on-the-ground events.
  • Briefs & cultural fit: tailored briefs per community — not translations.
  • Performance: tracking what converts, not what looks good.

Where I need help from the marketing team

  • Paid ads: Spark Ads and paid campaigns using creator content. CRM: promoting top creator videos to build trust with our existing base. Social media: complementary posts between creator drops and comment management.

What we'd measure

Conversion
  • Link clicks → WhatsApp conversations
  • First sends and 30-day repeat rate
  • CAC, measured in new senders within that period
  • Lift in new senders to the creator's corridor — DR creators drive DR, CO drives CO
Engagement
  • Views, likes, saves, shares, comments per creator
  • Average watch time, video retention rate, CPV, CPC
  • Comment sentiment (positive / neutral / negative)
  • Follower growth by region + engagement lift on Félix's other posts during the campaign
Traffic & brand lift
  • Branded search and direct type-ins to felixpago.com
  • Traffic from creator cities/zips in target metros (NY, FL, NJ, TX), with spikes on creator posting days
  • Searches for "remittances to DR/CO" + visits to corridor pages
Deep Dive 2 · DR-specific high-impact play

Field activation (DR focus)

How showing up in person helps us acquire users, convert them to a first transaction, and drive the second one quickly — in the corridor where digital alone won't break the habit.

Why this is the second deep dive
  • Acquisition: the DR community is older, more habit-driven, and uses the same remittance providers for years. Digital ads alone can't break that — you need to be present in their world.
  • First transaction: in-person guidance kills the fear of trying a new app. Someone walks you through the sign-up and you watch the money arrive in real time.
  • Second transaction: bodegas, barbershops and churches become recurring reminders. The community itself becomes the retention loop.
  • Why DR specifically: this is the biggest gap in Mexico's playbook (won online). DR needs hybrid — digital for scale, physical for trust.

Where:

Washington Heights The Bronx Paterson, NJ — highest-density Dominican areas in the U.S. Win here, word spreads.

What this actually looks like

Month 1

Research & outreach. Map the highest-density bodegas, barbershops, churches and high-traffic spots (subway exits, plazas) in Washington Heights, Bronx, Paterson NJ. Reach out to owners and community ambassadors. Brief a promoter agency for branded tents. Coordinate the first wave of locations, materials, QR codes and referral kits.

Month 2

Go live. Posters (QR → WhatsApp) in 20+ bodegas/area. Barbershops/salons activated — staff gets a referral QR code and a bonus per new account. Branded tents with promoters and flyers in high-traffic spots — assisted first send on the spot.

Month 3

Word of mouth becomes the engine. People keep coming back — promoters recognize them, ask how the first send went, push the second on the spot. Every satisfied user tells a neighbor. Top bodegas get permanent "Félix Point" signage. Track neighborhood-level repeat sends and referral rates.

How I'd measure this

QR clicks per location, first envíos and total envíos per neighborhood, repeat-send rate within 30 days, CAC measured per envío, and referrals generated by each ambassador. The bet: retention is stronger because trust is built in person.

What I own

  • Mapping locations, recruiting ambassadors and bodega/barbershop partners.
  • Promoter agency, tents, on-site execution and the assisted first-send experience.
  • Tracking by neighborhood, iterating on what converts.
  • Kits & merch: branded kits sent to ambassadors and partners so they feel part of the brand.

Where I need help from the marketing team

  • Paid ads: geo-targeted to the activation zones so customers get touched multiple times before and after the visit.
  • Social media: feature our ambassadors, announce where we'll be on the ground, and post photos from each activation.
  • Kits & merch: branded kits sent to ambassadors and partners so they feel part of the brand.
Deep Dive 3 · Time-sensitive cultural moments

Cultural & community events

Show up where the community gathers in moments of high cultural pride and emotion — when people are most connected to home and most open to sending money. The next big dates are weeks away.

Why this is the third deep dive — and why now
  • Colombian Independence Week (Jul 20) triggers 8+ festivals across the U.S. in a single week — the biggest CO moment of the year. Planning starts now.
  • Dominican Day Parade NYC (Aug 9) — 400K+ attendees, the largest single DR gathering in the U.S.
  • Cultural events are the only initiative with hard deadlines: miss the date and you wait a full year.
  • They cover all three goals: acquisition (massive foot traffic), first transaction (emotional pull + on-site assistance), second transaction (the moment becomes a memory tied to Félix).
🇩🇴 DR — key dates
  • Dominican Day Parade NYC — Aug 9 (400K+ attendees)
  • Dominican Parade NJ — Sep 13
  • LIDOM baseball season — Oct–Feb (cultural identity moment)
  • Navidad + Día de Reyes — Dec/Jan (high gift-send window)
  • Independencia Dominicana — Feb 27
🇨🇴 CO — key dates
  • Colombian Independence Week — Jul 20: 8+ festivals in one week (Chicago, Dallas, Houston, Miami, NYC, Fort Lauderdale)
  • Feria de Cali + Novenas — Dec 16–30
  • Regional patron-saint festivals throughout the year (costeños, valluneos, paisas)

Not just the big events — the small ones too

Big moments

Parades, national festivals, independence days. Massive reach, brand-building, photo ops with creators on-site. Best paired with paid social amplification.

Small gatherings

Neighborhood fairs, local sports leagues, cultural nights, church events. Lower cost, higher trust, direct conversations. The conversion engine.

Why it works

Félix is a digital product. Seeing a real human behind the brand at a moment of cultural pride creates trust no screen can match.

Month 1 · Lock the calendar

Plan & secure

  • Lock Colombian Independence Week (Jul 20) first — 8+ festivals, one week, biggest CO moment.
  • Secure sponsorships, booths, or activations at top 3 DR + top 3 CO events.
  • Coordinate with creators (Deep Dive 1) to attend events — live content multiplies reach.
  • Design the booth experience: assisted first-send, free WhatsApp send, small gift, photo moment.
Month 2 · Activate

Show up & convert

  • Jul 20 push — on-site activations across all 8+ festivals + creator content + paid amplification the same week.
  • Onsite reps walk attendees through their first transfer; recipient gets the money before they leave the booth.
  • Capture content at every event — reuse on paid, social, and CRM.
  • Track sign-ups by event with unique QR codes per location.
Month 3 · Build the calendar

Make it ongoing

  • Prep Dominican Day Parade NYC (Aug 9) — 400K+ attendees, the biggest single DR moment.
  • Build a year-round event calendar by corridor with owners and budgets.
  • Identify recurring small gatherings to attend monthly, not just annually.
  • Tx2 nudges referencing the event ("we met you at the parade — ready to send again?").

What I own

  • Calendar & prioritization: which events, which corridors, which dates win.
  • Partnerships: sponsors, organizers, community leaders, on-site creators.
  • Booth experience & on-the-ground execution.

Where I need help from the marketing team

  • Paid: geo-targeted ads around event dates and locations.
  • Social media: live coverage, Reels, Stories, post-event recaps.
  • CRM: pre-event "see you there" + post-event "we met you at" sequences.
